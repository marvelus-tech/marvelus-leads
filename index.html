#1 The "Only Script You Need"
Problem → Agitate → Present → Close

Opener: "Hi [Name], quick question — are you currently getting leads from Google for [service] in [City]?"

Problem: "Most [industry] businesses I talk to in [City] tell me the same thing: they rely on word-of-mouth or paid ads, but when someone searches '[service] near me' on their phone, they're not showing up."

Agitate: "Those people searching on Google are actively looking to buy. If you're not there, your competitor is. And they're getting that call."

Present: "What we do is get [industry] businesses to show up when people search for exactly what you offer. Not ads — organic Google results. People trust those more, and you don't pay per click."

Close: "I'm not asking for a commitment today. What I'd love to do is send you a free video audit of your site — shows exactly why you're not ranking and what it would take to fix it. What's the best email?"

#2 Pain-First Script
Let them pick their pain

"I work with [industry] businesses in [City] to solve three common problems:

  1. Not showing up when people search '[service] near me'
  2. Website doesn't convert visitors into calls
  3. Paying too much for leads that go to 3-4 competitors

Which of those, if any, is something you're dealing with right now?"

OBJ Objection Handling

"I already have SEO"

"Good — most of our best clients had someone before us. Quick question: are you getting 10+ calls/month from that relationship? [If no] Most owners don't know if their SEO is actually working because no one shows them the numbers."

"How much does it cost?"

"It depends on where you're starting. What I do first is a free audit — shows exactly what needs work. Then I can give you a real number, not a guess. Worth doing the audit?"

"I don't have budget"

"Totally get it. I'll send you the free audit anyway — shows you exactly what's costing you leads. Even if we don't work together, you'll know what to fix when budget opens up. What's the best email?"

"Send me an email"

"Happy to. Quick question so I send something useful — are you currently getting leads from Google, or is it mostly referrals? [Wait] Perfect. I'll tailor the audit around that."

Key Tips from Reddit Pros

• Never say "SEO" in opener — say "get found on Google"

• Lead with specific pain — "3-4 leads a week"

• Offer value first — free audit, video walkthrough

• Use numbers — "60-90 days", "8-12 calls"

• Remove risk — "no contracts", "month-to-month"

• Ask for easy yes — email is easier than meeting

• Follow up — 80% of sales happen after 5+ touchpoints

• Speak slowly — "almost uncomfortably slow"

• Stand up — use a standing desk for energy

• Record calls — listen and improve

Marketing Tactics That Work

• Free Video Audits (Loom) — 30-40% response rate

• Google Business Profile Optimization — free trial, 50%+ convert

• Review Generation Campaign — "10 new Google reviews this month — free"

• Local SEO Workshops — free 30-min Zoom, 10-15% convert

• Case Study Content — "How [Business] Got 12 More Calls/Month"
